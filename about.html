About

I'm a designer based between Rome and Milan, working at the intersection of research, technology, and lived experience. I hold an MSc in Digital & Interaction Design from Politecnico di Milano and a BA in Graphic Design & Art Direction from NABA Roma. My practice moves between screen and space, and I'm most at home where the digital meets the physical, the body, and the more-than-human.

What I do. I design research-driven physical–digital experiences, from UX strategy and interactive prototyping to brand systems and content. My work treats the digital as a passage, not a destination: interfaces meant to enrich what happens off-screen, in the body, in the world. I work across the full arc, from messy early thinking to polished delivery, and I care as much about what a thing means as how it looks.

What I care about. Mental health and wellness, and the design of interfaces that reduce friction and restore calm. Movement and the body as a site of interaction. Ecology and the systems we inhabit and shape, including the non-human ones. I'm drawn to slow, intentional design: spaces and products that respect attention, leave room to breathe, and make room for what doesn't usually get a seat at the table.
